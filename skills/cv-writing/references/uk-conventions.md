# UK CV Conventions (2025)

## Length

| Experience level | Target length |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Graduate (0–2 years) | 1 page |
| Mid-level (3–6 years) | 2 pages |
| Senior / Staff (7+ years) | 2–3 pages |

Two pages is the UK standard for anyone with 3+ years of experience. This is the
**opposite** of US convention (where 1 page dominates until senior level). Never
pad to hit 2 pages — if the content fills only 1.5 pages, that is fine.

---

## Personal Details — What to Include and Exclude

**Include:**
- Full name (larger font, top of document)
- City and country only — e.g. "London, UK" or "Manchester, UK" (never a full postal address)
- Phone number with international prefix: `+44 7XXX XXXXXX`
- Professional email address
- LinkedIn URL (shortened: `linkedin.com/in/username`)
- GitHub URL (if relevant): `github.com/username`
- Personal site or portfolio URL (optional)

**Exclude — hard rule:**
- Photo (illegal basis for discrimination under Equality Act 2010; most ATS strip them)
- Date of birth
- Marital status
- Nationality
- National Insurance number
- Full postal address
- "References available on request" (assumed; wastes space)

## Layout and Typography

- **Column structure**: single-column only. Two-column "designer" templates routinely break ATS parsing.
- **Font**: sans-serif throughout — Calibri, Arial, Helvetica, or Inter
- **Body text**: 10–11pt
- **Section headings**: 12–14pt, bold
- **Margins**: 1.5–2cm on all sides
- **Line spacing**: 1.0–1.15 within bullets; 1.5–2pt space between sections
- **Colour**: black text on white background for body. A single accent colour (dark navy, dark teal) for the name or section dividers is acceptable; never use colour for body text.

## UK English Spelling

Use UK spellings throughout. Mixed spelling is a common tell of a recycled US template.

| US spelling | UK spelling |
|------------|-------------|
| optimize | optimise |
| specialize | specialise |
| organization | organisation |
| behavior | behaviour |
| color | colour |
| analyze | analyse |
| center | centre |
| modeling | modelling |
| labeled | labelled |
| favor | favour |
| recognize | recognise |
| defense | defence |
| license (verb) | licence (noun) / license (verb) |
| program (generic) | programme (plan/schedule) |
| gray | grey |

When in doubt, use a UK dictionary (Oxford English Dictionary) as the reference.

## File Format

- **Default**: text-selectable PDF (`Firstname-Lastname-CV.pdf`)
- **Backup**: `.docx` — some older ATS instances parse Word more reliably than PDF
- Modern ATS (Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workable) handle well-formed, text-selectable PDFs
- **Never submit**: a scanned image of a CV, a `.pages` file, or a password-protected PDF
- File name must not contain spaces: `Alex-Smith-CV.pdf` not `Alex Smith CV.pdf`

---

## Contact Line Format

Place all contact details on a single line directly beneath the name, separated by `|` or `·`:

```
Alex Smith
London, UK · +44 7700 900000 · alex@example.com · linkedin.com/in/alexsmith · github.com/alexsmith
```

Do **not** put contact details in a document header or footer — many ATS parsers ignore header/footer regions entirely.

---

## Date Format

Use `MMM YYYY – MMM YYYY` throughout, consistent across all roles:

```
Jan 2022 – Mar 2024     ✓
01/2022 – 03/2024       ✗
January 2022 – present  ✓
2022–2024               ✗ (too vague; omits months)
```

For current roles: `Jan 2024 – Present`

---

## US-Headquartered Companies with UK Offices

Some US-headquartered scale-ups with London offices (e.g. Stripe, OpenAI London, Anthropic London)
effectively operate on US resume conventions even for UK roles. Check the careers-page tone:
- If the job listing uses "resume" rather than "CV", or specifies "1 page" — switch to US conventions (1 page, US spelling, "Summary" instead of "Profile", omit degree class)
- Otherwise, default to UK conventions above
